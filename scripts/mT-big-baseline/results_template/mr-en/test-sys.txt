The Japanese work culture is more categorical and formal, which can be similar to that of Westerners.
The suit is a standard dress of the business and the employees are called to each other by their surname or at the expense of their work.
It is very difficult to communicate at work, and it is more important to focus on collective efforts than on personal achievements.
The workers often need the approval of the elders for any decision taken by the workers and they also need to follow the advice of their seniors without any question.
