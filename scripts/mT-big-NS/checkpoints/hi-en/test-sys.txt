The Japanese work culture is more graded and formal than the Western ones.
Suits are standard professional garments, and co-workers call each other by their family names or post names.
Workplace harmony is important, which emphasizes collective effort rather than appreciation of individual achievements.
Workers are often required to take the approval of their senior officials for any decision, and are required to follow the instructions of their seniors without question.
